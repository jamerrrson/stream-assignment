What are Streaming Services?

Streaming services are an online provider of entertainment that is delivered through the internet to a person’s computer, mobile device or television. The purpose of streaming services is to provide an alternative to cable television and on demand services that are typically offered at a lower cost.[5] The way it functions is that the consumer pays a fee and gains access to a variety of TV shows and movies on the streaming platform that they can watch whenever they please. Subscribers of the service pay a monthly fee to maintain the ability to view content on the service.

Some major features of Streaming Services:

  1. Able to watch content whenever and whereever
  2. TV Shows usually have complete seasons at launch, in comparison to free-to-air which in which the consumer has to wait for episodes
  3. Wide variety for consumers

Who developed Streaming Services

The first well renown successful attempt at live-streaming was in 1993 in which an American rock band “Severe Tire Damage” live-streamed their performance over the internet using Xerox PARC’s “Mbone”; this was a massive success as people as far as Australia we’re able to view the live-stream.[1]

The streaming service technology was not invented by one set group, rather a culmination of various companies technological advancements. The first documented commercial streaming product was “StarWorks” (1992), which allowed for ‘on-demand’ MPEG-1 videos to be streamed via ethernet services. The creation of streaming products were created for the sole purpose of multishare video streaming for anyone with direct access to these streaming services.[1]Nine-teen years ago, China-based Hong Kong Telecom (HKT) launched the world’s first video-on-demand service, as part of a wider interactive television (iTV) service. With the iTV VOD service, subscribers could watch over a hundred local and foreign movies, cartoons and documentaries. [9]

What groups are responsible for managing them?

There are almost no government bodies responsible for the monitoring of streaming services. ACMA’s Broadcasting Act of 1992 does not fully affect the streaming industry [2]. The corporations who invented the various streaming services are the ones responsible for the content shown.

Predicting the future of Streaming Services

From its birth in the early 1990’s, and the creation of streaming services such as Netflix, Disney+, Stan, Binge, and various video-game streaming services such as Twitch and Youtube- the industry has boomed. According to SurveyMonkey, roughly 27% of participants in their survey aged 18-29 prefer streaming services. This is in comparison to the 16% who solely prefer broadcast Television; the rest watch both.[3] Due to this boom, and the rapid decline in popularity of Broadcast television, there will be a time in which the majority of news and television will be consumed via streaming services.

What are related technologies?

Related technologies is something such as Youtube which is a streaming service, however, the content that is uploaded onto the website is not studio quality films or TV shows. It is instead videos that are user generated and involves much more interaction with comments being posted onto the video for direct and instant feedback, something that isn’t involved with streaming services such as Netflix.

Definitions:

Streaming:
Streaming refers to any media content – live or recorded – delivered to computers and mobile devices via the internet and played back in real time. [8]
Service:
Service is defined as someone or something that is intended to provide help to those providing assistance to others. [7]
Subscription:
A subscription is an amount of money that you pay regularly in order to belong to an organization. [6]
Streaming Service:
An online provider of entertainment (music, movies, etc.) that delivers the content via an Internet connection to the subscriber's computer, TV or mobile device. [4]
